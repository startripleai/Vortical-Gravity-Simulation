# 🌌 The Structural Regulation of Galactic Thickness

## 1. Executive Summary
In the **Vortical Gravity** framework, the 3D geometry of a galaxy—specifically the thickness of its stellar and gaseous disk—is not a result of random velocity dispersion. Instead, it is a **Topological Equilibrium** between the restorative **Expansion Pressure ($P_{EP}$)** of the spacetime lattice and the localized damping of the gravitational constant ($G$).

## 2. The Vertical Density-Efficiency Gradient
A galactic disk represents a high-density 2D structure embedded in a 3D vacuum. This creates a sharp vertical gradient in **Lattice Stress ($X$)**:

*   **Mid-plane (High Density):** The concentration of baryonic mass "clogs" the lattice nodes, triggering the **Suppression Factor ($S \approx 4.46$)**. This reduces the local gravitational coupling toward the terrestrial baseline ($G_0$).
*   **Vertical Fringes (Low Density):** Moving away from the mid-plane along the $z$-axis, the density drops precipitously. The lattice "un-damps," and gravity restores toward its unsuppressed vacuum potential ($G_{max} \approx 5.46 G_0$).

## 3. The Self-Correcting Feedback Loop
The thickness ($z$) of a disk is regulated by a mechanical feedback mechanism inherent in the discrete tensor network:

1.  **Compression:** If the disk collapses further, mid-plane density ($\rho$) rises, causing $X$ to increase. This further **suppresses** gravity ($G \to G_0$).
2.  **Repulsion:** As gravity weakens due to damping, the internal **Expansion Pressure ($P_{EP}$)**—generated by the lattice's mandatory state-transitions—becomes dominant.
3.  **Restoration:** This pressure pushes the baryonic mass outward vertically until it reaches a height where the restored vacuum gravity ($G_{max}$) can contain it.

## 4. Empirical Observation: "Puffy" Dwarf Galaxies
This model provides a first-principles explanation for the structural differences between galaxy types:

*   **Milky Way (Dense):** High mid-plane density keeps the lattice damped ($G \approx G_0$), allowing for a relatively thin, highly compressed disk.
*   **M33 (Diffuse):** Low overall density means the lattice remains largely **Un-damped ($G \to 5.46$)** even near the center. The high internal **Expansion Pressure** in this low-density regime pushes the gas further out vertically, resulting in the "puffy" or "fluffy" appearance characteristic of gas-rich dwarf spirals.
